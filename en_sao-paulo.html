Known as the “concrete jungle,” this city has more than 12 million inhabitants and is considered the main economic and cultural center of Latin America.

Rich in history and diversity, the city offers an endless number of museums, iconic parks, and a cuisine that ranks among the best in the world. However, its climate is famous for its instability, and it is common to experience all four seasons in the same day under the classic “paulistana drizzle.”

Museum

Ipiranga Museum

A symbol of Brazil’s Independence, the Ipiranga Museum preserves national memory within an imposing neoclassical building surrounded by French-style gardens.

museudoipiranga.org.br
Science Park

The Instituto Butantan

The Instituto Butantan is responsible for producing most of the sera used in Brazil against venomous and poisonous animals. It is the largest producer of vaccines and sera in Latin America and the leading producer of immunobiological products in Brazil. Its efficiency and quality have made it a global reference.

butantan.org.br
Park

Ibirapuera Park

São Paulo’s green lung, Ibirapuera Park blends nature, leisure, and Oscar Niemeyer’s iconic architecture in one place.

parquedoibirapuera.org
Museum

MASP — Museum of Art of São Paulo

MASP is an icon of modern architecture that houses the most important collection of European art in the Southern Hemisphere.
